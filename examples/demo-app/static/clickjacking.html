Clickjacking  Medium Mitigated by VibeWarden

An attacker embeds your page in a transparent iframe over a fake button. Users think they are clicking the attacker's UI, but they are actually clicking your protected page — triggering actions they never intended.

What is Clickjacking?

Clickjacking (UI Redress Attack) tricks users into clicking on something different from what they perceive. The attacker loads the target site in a transparent or semi-transparent iframe, then overlays a decoy interface on top. When the victim clicks the visible button (e.g. "Click to win a prize!"), they are actually clicking a button on the hidden page underneath.

Common targets include: payment confirmation pages, account deletion flows, "Like" buttons on social networks, and any action that requires a single click.

Attack steps

  1. Attacker creates a malicious page with an iframe pointing to the target site.
  2. The iframe is styled with opacity: 0.01 and positioned to overlap a decoy button.
  3. Victim visits the attacker's page and clicks what looks like an innocuous button.
  4. The click lands on the hidden iframe — triggering a real action on the target site.

VibeWarden Protection

X-Frame-Options: DENY and CSP frame-ancestors

VibeWarden adds two complementary headers to every response:

X-Frame-Options: DENY
Content-Security-Policy: ...; frame-ancestors 'none'

X-Frame-Options: DENY is the legacy header supported by all browsers. It instructs the browser to never render the response inside a frame, regardless of which site is trying to embed it.

frame-ancestors 'none' is the modern CSP equivalent. When both are present, all browsers are covered. You can verify the headers are set by checking the Headers Inspector.

Zero configuration required — VibeWarden applies these headers automatically.
